JavaScript Console Methods

  • console.assert(expression, message)   Writes an error message to the console if the assertion is false.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.clear()   Clears the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.count()   Logs the number of times that this particular call to count() has been called.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.error()   Outputs an error message to the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.log(message)   Outputs a message to the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.group()   Creates a new inline group in the console. This indents following console messages by an additional level, until console.groupEnd() is called.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.groupCollapsed()   Creates a new inline group in the console. However, the new group is created collapsed. The user will need to use the disclosure button to expand it.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.groupEnd())   Exits the current inline group in the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.info(message)   Outputs an informational message to the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.table(tabledata, tablecolumns)   Displays tabular data as a table.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.time()   Starts a timer (can track how long an operation takes).

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.timeEnd()   Stops a timer that was previously started by console.time().

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.

  • console.warn(message)   Outputs a warning message to the console.

    Example:

    View in console or press F12 on your keyboard to view the message in the console view.
